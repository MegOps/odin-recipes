The Simple Scrambled Eggs

Description

Scrambled eggs are a classic breakfast dish made by whisking together eggs and cooking them in a pan until they are cooked through and slightly fluffy. To make scrambled eggs, the eggs are typically cracked into a bowl, beaten until the yolks and whites are fully combined, and seasoned with salt and pepper or other seasonings of your choice. The eggs are then poured into a hot, greased pan and stirred constantly with a spatula until they begin to solidify. Some people like their scrambled eggs cooked until they are completely set, while others prefer them a bit runny. Scrambled eggs can be made in a variety of ways, from plain and simple to fancy and loaded with toppings such as cheese, herbs, vegetables, and meats. They are a quick and easy breakfast option that can be enjoyed on their own or as part of a larger breakfast spread.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon milk (optional)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs until they are well beaten.
  2. In a non-stick skillet over medium heat, melt the butter.
  3. Add the eggs to the skillet and use a spatula to stir them constantly until they start to set.
  4. If using, add the milk to the skillet and continue stirring the eggs until they are cooked through and no longer runny.
  5. Remove the skillet from the heat and season the eggs with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Transfer the scrambled eggs to a plate and serve immediately.
  7. Enjoy your delicious scrambled eggs!
